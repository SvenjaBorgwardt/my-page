§ 01 — FEATURED WORK

THREE PROJECTS.
ONE THESIS.

Each of these came from something that was already broken in my classroom. The thesis under all three: AI should be addressed to the learner, not at them — and sometimes the most useful thing it can do is less, not more.

§01 / KALEIDO

Kaleido

A tool for giving individual feedback on student writing. Because with 240 students a week, proper individual feedback was simply not possible.

Names stripped locally, anonymised by agent, analysed along separate dimensions — structure, evidence, language, coherence, grammar, content. A final agent writes the feedback, addressed to the student rather than about them.

"Students came to me afterwards with questions about their own essays. Not about the mark. About the essay itself."

GDPR- and EU AI Act-compliant. Mistral, because EU jurisdiction matters when you handle German school data.

 §02 / COMPASS

Compass

Direction, not destination — a model that helps without handing over the answer.

AI creates a specific failure of self-assessment: students read the quality of the output as evidence of their own learning. The illusion of fluency. Socratic agents annoy actual learners. I want something in between.

STUDENT: "It says syntax error."
STANDARD: "You're missing a colon."
WHAT I WANT: "Read the error again. Look at line 1."
Mistral 7B · LoRA · DPOFine-tuning
 §03 / UTE

UTE

A voice-first ordering system for bakery counters. Built at the BÄKO Hackathon with three other women. We won first place.

The assistant keeps talking to the customer. UTE listens, transcribes, matches, and builds the order silently. When the assistant repeats it back — which she'd do anyway — that serves as confirmation. Nothing gets clicked.

"What if the best way for an AI to be useful is to disappear?"

§04 / CLAUDIA

Claudia

My multi-agent infrastructure. She runs locally on a Mac Mini that's always on in my flat.

By day she orchestrates the agents behind Kaleido. By night she runs my home automation. Same framework, different endpoints. Most of what I know about agent design, memory, and orchestration I learned by building her — and then watching her break in interesting ways.

Mac Mini · Multi-agentAlways on
 §05 / INVESTOR GAME

Investor Game

A classroom platform where students develop product ideas, pitch them to the group, and invest play money into each other's pitches.

Scoring is part quality-of-pitch, part performance-of-portfolio. You're rewarded both for being a good founder and for being a good judge of other founders. I wrote it because nothing off the shelf handled that combination. I've never seen market economics taken more seriously.
