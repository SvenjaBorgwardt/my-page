01 · KALEIDO

Kaleido, or how feedback
came back addressed.

A tool for individual feedback on student writing. With 240 students a week, proper individual feedback was simply not possible. Kaleido is what I made instead.

Names stripped locally, anonymised by agent, analysed along separate dimensions — structure, evidence, language, coherence, grammar, content. A final agent writes the feedback, addressed to the student rather than about them.

GDPR- and EU AI Act-compliant, because EU jurisdiction matters when you handle German school data.

Students came to me with questions about their own essays. Not about the mark. About the essay itself.

02 · COMPASS

Compass, or the problem
with the easy answer.

AI creates a specific failure of self-assessment: the student reads the quality of the output as evidence of their own learning. Cognitive science calls this the illusion of fluency.

For something to be learned, it has to be slightly harder than easy — Robert Bjork's desirable difficulties. Socratic agents are the obvious counter-move. They annoy actual learners. I want something in between.

Example · A coding bug
Student"My code doesn't work: for i in range(10) print(i)"
Standard"You're missing a colon. Here's the fix."
Socratic"What is a for loop? What is syntax?"
What I want"Read the error message again, then look at the end of line 1."

Right now I'm fine-tuning Mistral 7B with LoRA and DPO. Goal: a model genuinely useful for English class — specifically for debating.

A compass points the way. It doesn't hand you the map.

03 · UTE

UTE, or the AI that
chose to disappear.

A voice-first ordering system for bakery counters. Built in a weekend at the BÄKO Hackathon with three other women. We won first place.

The assistant keeps talking to the customer. UTE listens, transcribes, matches items, builds the order silently. When the assistant repeats the order back — which she'd do anyway — that serves as confirmation. Nothing gets clicked.

What if the best way for an AI to be useful is to disappear?

UTE · Unkomplizierte Theken-Eingabe · uncomplicated counter input · also a common German name.
